Reading could help you live longer. People who read books live for around two years longer than those who don't, irrespective of gender, wealth, education or health, according to a Yale University study.

While a similar association was found among readers of newspapers, it was weaker. “Book reading contributed to a survival advantage that was significantly greater than that observed for reading newspapers or magazines,” the authors of the study wrote. “Compared to non-book readers, book readers had a 23-month survival advantage,” they noted. Deep reading promotes empathy and emotional intelligence - cognitive processes that can lead to greater chance of survival, the report explained.
